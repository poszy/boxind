What not to do when printing

#1 We Cannot screen print Pictures

#2 Never print really close to seams

When printing on t-shirts and v-necks, we never recommend printing near the seams. Printing really near the seams will cause ink buildup on the screen and thus the part of the design that is really near the seam will appear really blotchy and not proportionate to the rest of the print on the shirt. The reason this happens is because the deams are thicker material than the rest of the shrit and does not lay flat on our pallet (pallets are the boards we use to load the shirts onto our printing press) by not laying flat it causes a bigger gap from teh screen to the shirt causing a heavier ink deposit in that area than the rest of the shirt.

#3 Never Print on the seams!

printing on or over the seam is also not a good idea due to the thicker material of the seam. when pritning on teh seam the seam borders willl have uneven prints due to the increased gaps from teh screen to that area, the printed surface on top of the screen will due to the pressure of the squeegee going over a surface that is too close to it.

#4 Do not print on ribbed shirts

Although ribbed clothing may offer a great fitting experience, it does not offer a good printing experience. The problem with ribbed garments is very simlar to printing on or near seams, the ripples that go down the shirt are smaller verions of seams. So essentially you are printing over several small seams, meaning that the gaps or holes inbetween the ripples will either be filled with excess ink deposits or they will not be filled with enoughink. The problem with printing with ribbed shirts is that when it comes to wearing the finished product the ripples will stretch out causing the print to appear 'cracked' due to the non flat surface of the shirt. Also ribbed shirts are made of a material that rejects the ink that is being printed on it.

#5 We Preffer not to print garments that are mainly polyester based

when printing on garments we prefer shirts that are not over a 50 /50 blend with the exception of hoodies, we preffer our hoodies to be at teh most a 80% cotton / 20% polyester to reduce the chances of dye migration

What is dye migration? dye migration is the incident where polyerst based material eats through tWEhe printed color of the ink. this causes the layers of ink printed to eventually become transparent and eventually becoming the same color as the material being printed on or have a 'milky' looking finish.

if you choose not to use the garments we provide for you to use please pay attention to the labels to make sure that the garment is mailnly made of cotton fabric.

#6 Do Not print on double lined fabric

The problem with double lined fabrics si that our adhesive is unable to stick to the print surface because of teh second layer of fabric underneath it. having a second layer of fabric underneat it the print surface can slide around on the paller causing a 3# looking misprint.
